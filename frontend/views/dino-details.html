Tyrannosaurus rex

(***Tyrant Lizard King***)

Overview & Key Features

T. rex was an **apex predator** of its time, known for its massive skull, heavy tail, and serrated teeth capable of crushing bone. Its discovery was a turning point in paleontology, solidifying the image of the dinosaur as a dynamic, powerful creature.

**Size & Speed:** Despite its bulk, recent research suggests it could run up to 25 mph. Its small forelimbs remain one of the great mysteries of its anatomy.

Plesiosaurus

(***Near Lizard***)

Overview & Key Features

Plesiosaurus was a marine reptile (not strictly a dinosaur) known for its broad body and **four large flippers**, suggesting it moved through the water using an underwater 'flying' motion. Its most distinct feature was its extremely long neck.

**Habitat:** A successful marine predator found in the seas of the Jurassic period, often preying on fish and cephalopods.

Pterosaurus (Pterodactyl)

(***Winged Lizard***)

Overview & Key Features

Pterosaurs were the **first vertebrates known to fly** (not dinosaurs, but flying reptiles). They were characterized by a wing formed by a membrane of skin, muscle, and other tissues stretching from the ankles to a dramatically lengthened fourth finger.

**Size & Flight:** Size varied wildly, from sparrow-sized to giant forms like Quetzalcoatlus with 10-meter wingspans. They were highly adapted flyers with hollow bones.

Stegosaurus

(***Roofed Lizard***)

Overview & Key Features

Stegosaurus is instantly recognizable by its row of kite-like **bony plates (scutes)** running along its back and the dangerous **spiked tail (thagomizer)** used for defense. Despite its size, it possessed a surprisingly small brain.

**Defense:** The plates may have been used for display or thermoregulation (body temperature control), while the thagomizer was a lethal defensive weapon against predators.
